Experiment 2: Vector Art, Animation & Interactivity

Description

What are you trying to accomplish with this experiment?

In this experiment, I am trying to create an animated, psychedelic visual of concentric rectangles that oscillate between a minimum and maximum size.

The rectangles will rotate and have rapidly and randomly changing colors, creating a colorful spiral effect.

This experiment uses p5.js to manipulate shapes and animations while exploring transformations, color modes, and interactivity within the canvas.

Technical

Describe in detail what you did technically and how it works?

Technically, I used p5.js to animate concentric rectangles that grow and shrink, creating a visually dynamic effect.

The 'a' variable controls the size of the outer rectangle, while a 'direction' variable toggles between growing and shrinking.

I used 'translate()'' to center the drawing and `rotate()` to apply rotations to the rectangles.

Multiple nested rectangles are drawn with decreasing sizes using a loop, creating a spiral effect.

I also utilized the HSB color mode to give the rectangles vibrant and randomly changing colors.

The size of the rectangles oscillates between 'minSize' and 'maxSize'.

Reflection

Each person that worked on the code should reflect on the process, the difficulties, and the successes of the experiment.

Hasina Reflection: The process involved experimenting with p5.js to create animated shapes. However, I faced many difficulties with getting the growth and shrinkage of the rectangles to work smoothly.

Initially, I think I accidentally updating the rectangle's size too quickly, but after adjusting the update logic, the oscillating behavior was fixed.

Another challenge was managing the color changes and ensuring they were applied correctly across the shapes.

The success was that I was able to integrate transformations, animations, and interactivity to achieve a vibrant, animated spiral effect with a rectangle.
